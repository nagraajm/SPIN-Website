Research Areas

Our multidisciplinary research spans across various aspects of spinal pain management and intervention

Clinical Research

Conducting randomized controlled trials and clinical studies to evaluate the efficacy of spinal intervention techniques.

  • Pain outcome assessments
  • Functional improvement studies
  • Long-term safety analysis

Imaging Research

Advanced imaging techniques and AI-assisted diagnosis for precise spinal intervention guidance.

  • MRI-guided procedures
  • Ultrasound innovation
  • AI diagnostic tools

Biomechanics

Understanding spinal mechanics and movement patterns to optimize treatment approaches.

  • Motion analysis
  • Load distribution studies
  • Postural assessments

Molecular Research

Investigating cellular and molecular mechanisms underlying spinal pain and degeneration.

  • Inflammatory pathways
  • Biomarker discovery
  • Regenerative medicine

Technology Innovation

Developing next-generation devices and digital health solutions for spinal care.

  • Device development
  • Digital therapeutics
  • Telemedicine platforms

Outcome Research

Measuring and analyzing treatment outcomes to improve patient care and clinical decision-making.

  • Patient-reported outcomes
  • Cost-effectiveness analysis
  • Quality of life studies
